About Heart Disease


🫀 Overview of Heart Disease

Heart disease refers to a range of conditions that affect the heart’s structure and function. The most common form is Coronary Artery Disease (CAD), which occurs when fatty deposits (plaque) build up in the arteries that supply blood to the heart. This reduces oxygen flow, leading to chest pain, shortness of breath, and sometimes a heart attack.
Early detection and healthy habits can greatly reduce the risk.






❤ Types of Heart Disease


Coronary Artery Disease (CAD) – Blockage or narrowing of heart arteries due to plaque buildup.
Heart Attack (Myocardial Infarction) –Occurs when blood flow to part of the heart is completely blocked.
Arrhythmia – Irregular or abnormal heartbeat.
Heart Failure – The heart becomes weak and cannot pump blood properly.
Cardiomyopathy – Disease of the heart muscle that affects its pumping ability.
Heart Valve Disease – Valves do not open or close properly, affecting blood flow.

⚠ Risk Factors


High blood pressure (hypertension) – Damages artery walls and forces the heart to work harder.
High cholesterol levels – Leads to plaque buildup that narrows and stiffens arteries.
Diabetes or high blood sugar – Damages blood vessels and accelerates atherosclerosis.
Smoking and alcohol consumption – Smoking injures vessel lining; excess alcohol raises BP and triglycerides.
Obesity or overweight – Increases blood pressure, cholesterol, and risk of diabetes.
Lack of regular physical activity – Reduces cardiovascular fitness and contributes to weight gain.
Family history of heart disease – Genetic factors can elevate baseline risk at an earlier age.
Stress and poor diet – Raises blood pressure and inflammation; high salt/sugar/fats worsen risk.

💔 Common Symptoms


Chest pain, heaviness, or pressure (angina) – Discomfort that often worsens with exertion and eases with rest.
Shortness of breath, especially during activity – Breathlessness due to reduced oxygen delivery to tissues.
Fatigue or weakness – Low cardiac output can cause persistent tiredness.
Dizziness or fainting – May result from abnormal rhythms or a sudden drop in blood pressure.
Pain in the neck, jaw, back, or arms – Referred pain that can accompany heart attack, especially in women.
Cold sweats, nausea, or indigestion – Autonomic response frequently seen during acute coronary events.



Technology & Approach


Datasets Analysed

UCI Heart Disease (Cleveland): A widely used benchmark dataset with diverse patient features and outcomes.
Framingham Heart Study: The foundational longitudinal dataset for cardiovascular risk, providing decades of comprehensive patient follow-up.

Referenced Research

A Comparative Study of Machine Learning Algorithms for Heart Disease Prediction: A widely cited survey benchmarking state-of-the-art machine learning approaches on structured heart disease datasets, emphasizing predictive value and interpretability.
Framingham Risk Score Original Paper: Introduced the classic multivariate risk score methodology, now foundational in both clinical practice and cardiovascular research.

Algorithms Used

Logistic Regression, Naive Bayes, SVM, KNN, Decision Tree, Random Forest, XGBOOST, Neural Network

Key Parameters

Major features: Chest Pain Type (cp), Resting Blood Pressure (trestbps), Serum Cholesterol (chol), Fasting Blood Sugar (fbs), Resting ECG (restecg), Maximum Heart Rate Achieved (thalach), Oldpeak

Rapid Deployment

System designed for quick setup and integration, enabling timely implementation in real-world medical environments.

Explainable Results

Outputs are transparent and interpretable, supporting clinical decision-making and enhancing trust in predictive analytics.
