Types of Heart Disease

  • Coronary Artery Disease (CAD): Blockage or narrowing of heart arteries due to plaque buildup.

  • Heart Attack (Myocardial Infarction): Occurs when blood flow to part of the heart is completely blocked.

  • Arrhythmia: Irregular or abnormal heartbeat.

  • Heart Failure: The heart becomes weak and cannot pump blood properly.

  • Cardiomyopathy: Disease of the heart muscle that affects its pumping ability.

  • Heart Valve Disease: Valves do not open or close properly, affectingÂ bloodÂ flow.


Risk Factors

Heart disease develops due to a combination of lifestyle, genetic, and health-related factors.

Common risk factors include:

  • High blood pressure (hypertension)
  • High cholesterol levels
  • Diabetes or high blood sugar
  • Smoking and alcohol consumption
  • Obesity or overweight
  • Lack of regular physical activity
  • Family history of heart disease
  • Stress and poor diet


Common Symptoms

Heart disease symptoms may vary from mild to severe. Some of the most common include:

  • Chest pain, heaviness, or pressure (angina)
  • Shortness of breath, especially during activity
  • Fatigue or weakness
  • Dizziness or fainting
  • Pain in the neck, jaw, back, or arms
  • Cold sweats, nausea, or indigestion
